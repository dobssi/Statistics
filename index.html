About This Repository

This repository contains rigorous statistical analyses of Swedish mortality data (2000-2025), with a focus on identifying and debunking misleading statistical claims circulating on social media.

All analyses use official Swedish and European mortality statistics, with transparent methodologies and full source attribution.

Data Sources

  • Swedish Official Statistics: The Health and Welfare Statistical Database (Hälso- och sjukvårdens statistikdatabas)
  • Eurostat: Weekly death statistics by age group
  • Methodology: Linear baselines (2010-2019), 95% prediction intervals, standard epidemiological methods

🔍 Debunking: Swedish Child Mortality Claims

February 12, 2026 | Ages 0-4 Analysis

Comprehensive debunking of claims that Swedish child mortality (ages 0-4) showed a "66% increase in 2024" and that "disease-related causes stopped declining."

Key Findings:

  • Claimed "66% increase" was actually an 8.7% decrease in total deaths
  • 2024 showed 242 deaths (52 below 2010-19 linear baseline, -17.7%)
  • Lowest year on record in entire 1997-2024 dataset
  • Exposes "54 causes" small number statistics fallacy
  • 2025 tracking even lower through Week 39

⚠️ Debunking: The "Increasing Blue Bands" Trick

February 16, 2026 | Statistical Manipulation Exposed

Analysis of a sophisticated visualization technique that creates the illusion of a mortality crisis by measuring "percentage of causes increasing" while total mortality actually declined.

The Deception:

  • Chart shows "more blue" (more causes increasing) while total mortality fell 15-18%
  • Ages 20-24: Actual mortality down 14.8% vs baseline
  • Ages 25-29: Actual mortality down 18.3% vs baseline
  • Mechanism: Major causes decline significantly, many tiny causes randomly increase
  • Includes "Coin Flip Crisis" analogy and detailed examples

Methodology

Baseline Selection: All analyses use 2010-2019 linear baselines to properly account for pre-existing declining mortality trends. Using flat averages would artificially inflate deficits in contexts where mortality is declining.

Statistical Approach: Linear regression on baseline period, 95% prediction intervals using t-distribution, accounting for both regression uncertainty and natural variation.

Transparency: All data sources are cited, methodologies are explained, and limitations are acknowledged. Charts and visualizations are embedded directly in reports for easy verification.

All analyses based on official Swedish and European statistical data
