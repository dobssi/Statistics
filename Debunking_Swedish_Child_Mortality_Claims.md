## THE "54 CAUSES" STATISTICAL MANIPULATION

### The Problem

The original thread claims to analyze "top 54 causes of death" for 0-4 year olds. With only **242 total deaths** in 2024, this creates a critical statistical problem:

**Deaths per category if evenly distributed: 242 ÷ 54 = 4.5 deaths**

### Reality of Cause Distribution

Death causes in young children are highly skewed:

**Major Categories (account for ~70% of deaths):**
- Congenital malformations: ~50-80 deaths/year
- Sudden Infant Death Syndrome: ~20-40 deaths/year
- Accidents/injuries: ~15-30 deaths/year
- Perinatal conditions: ~20-40 deaths/year

**Remaining 50+ Categories:**
- Most have **0-10 deaths per year** for the ENTIRE COUNTRY
- Many have **0-3 deaths per year**

### Small Number Statistics Fallacy

When analyzing categories with only 1-5 deaths per year:

| Scenario | 2023 Deaths | 2024 Deaths | % Change | Reality |
|----------|-------------|-------------|----------|---------|
| Rare Disease A | 2 | 5 | **+150%** | Just 3 extra cases |
| Rare Disease B | 1 | 3 | **+200%** | Just 2 extra cases |
| Rare Disease C | 0 | 2 | **+∞%** | Just 2 cases total |
| Rare Disease D | 3 | 1 | **-67%** | Just 2 fewer cases |

**The Manipulation:**
1. Divide 242 deaths across 54 tiny categories
2. Random variation creates massive percentage swings
3. Cherry-pick the categories that randomly increased
4. Aggregate them and claim "rapid growth"
5. **Ignore** the categories that randomly decreased
6. Present percentages without absolute numbers

This is **classic statistical malpractice**.

### Example: What "66% Growth" Would Actually Mean

If we take the claim at face value for a subset of causes:

**Scenario 1 - If applied to a small category:**
- 2023: 3 deaths

## APPENDIX: STATISTICAL NOTES

### Small Number Statistics

When dealing with rare events (n < 20 per category):

**Poisson Distribution Applies:**
- Expected variance = mean
- Standard error ≈ √n
- 95% CI very wide for small n

**Examples:**
- n=1: 95% CI ≈ [0.03, 5.6]
- n=2: 95% CI ≈ [0.2, 7.2]
- n=5: 95% CI ≈ [1.6, 11.7]

**Implication:** Changes of ±2-3 cases are **always** within expected random variation for rare causes.

### Multiple Comparison Problem

Testing 54 cause categories creates:
- 54 opportunities to find "significant" results
- Expected false positives: 54 × 0.05 = 2.7
- Required correction: Bonferroni α = 0.05/54 = 0.00093
- Or False Discovery Rate control

**Without correction:** Results are unreliable.

### Coefficient of Variation

For ages 0-4 total deaths (1997-2024):
- Mean: 328
- SD: 46
- CV: 14%

**This is high variance**, meaning year-to-year changes of ±10-15% are **normal fluctuation**, not meaningful trends.
